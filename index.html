What was it like being a woman back in the day? How would men react to sudden changes in status and liberty?

Food for thought, January 7, 2019, 12:05 pm


So this was a question that I posed to myself a while back.
It's something that has been eating me up for some time now, and to be honest-
all it does is open up some room for a heated argument if left under a complete
lack of supervision. Rather than spewing vulgar insults and baseless falsities
all throughout, I'd much prefer that we all lead the way for an open conversation!
So please don't bother trying to comment something sinister down below the comment
section. .

For starters, it would've been most torturous for us men if we were in a
woman's shoes back in the day ( in the early to mid-19th century to be exact ).
While that may seem blunt from the surface, evidence alone does indeed imply that
they were consistently alienated, shackled, and ridiculed all throughout their life,
and guess what! from that point onward, it's not even something as alleged as an implication,
it was their reality and their truth! I mean, have you people seen the sheer amount of documentaries
filmed over this stuff? Anyhow, I'm going off-topic here. My point was that men would probably despise
the lack of control they'd then have if they laced shut in a " female's place " or so society indicates.

Men were well known for their fragile egos and misplaced judgments of the average woman.
These judgments did indeed stem from their fear of losing control and authority over a woman;
moreover, some might argue that women would have wreaked havoc across left and right if they
were permitted to " run along scot-free " though truthfully speaking, they were nothing
if not terrified of such " major " loss of ego fuel.
Men back in the day would incessantly claim and reaffirm the belief that women were more
suited for house chores and raising children, though, in reality, their arguments were pointless
at best ( let us not delve further into that rabbit hole )

To finish things off: women still are and will always be the backbone of society,
whether they're versed in any industry or job. Chaining them down to the wheel misfortune
would do us no good! Cooperation is necessary, and we should always remind ourselves of that.

Feel free to disagree with anything stated! Though we must all keep things civil!!
